Finally, Scybernethics situates itself within broader fields opened by [Foucault](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/F6Y7ZDMBPVeP351bGB3b7Q/Foucault) and [Stiegler](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/uDKzDHdhlFy9s23HRcPYzw/Stiegler), where subjectivation, power, technics, and [ecology](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/SwvzTJsCz1i_pgK4u5WWtg/ecology) become central ethical terrains, and it resonates with more experiential and ontological motifs such as [felt meaning (Gendlin)](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/lWlEMcXSiF2maHhCP_SoXw/felt%20meaning%20%28Gendlin%29), [sunyata and “ignorance”](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/jZ19OjmvrF-QDHh6DnXAsg/sunyata%20and%20%E2%80%9Cignorance%E2%80%9D), and the dynamic of [poiésis and noesis](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/PSPO9N1i2lOoSJfdMiqRRg/poi%C3%A9sis%20and%20noesis).

### II. Rationalité²: Second‑Order Enactive Rationality

At the heart of Scybernethics lies [rationalité² circulatoire et interactive](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/O9epOr4e2VqG5nJUH2Ua5g/rationalit%C3%A9%C2%B2%20circulatoire%20et%20interactive), a form of rationality that no longer aspires to stand outside the systems it analyses, but rather acknowledges its own recursive involvement in them. Rationalité² is “quadratic” and homeostatic: it attends to circularities and feedbacks, and seeks coherence not by eliminating tension but by maintaining dynamic balances along multiple axes—cohérence–suspension (holding and bracketing judgements), raison–création (linking explanation and invention), incarnation–sens (binding formal structures to embodied meaning). Thoughts such as [double récursion et récursivité croisée](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/NODracDFR1yBCm_zK4n8ig/double%20r%C3%A9cursion%20et%20r%C3%A9cursivit%C3%A9%20crois%C3%A9e) and [l’inséparabilité de l’individuation et de la connaissance](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/_2Gw2qEbfF242iBgj7NsfQ/l%E2%80%99ins%C3%A9parabilit%C3%A9%20de%20l%E2%80%99individuation%20et%20de%20la%20connaissance) emphasize that knowing is itself an individuating and transformative process, under conditions of [auto‑régulation et autonomie](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/K0jXNoYHkV-uuuSszqPYog/auto%E2%80%91r%C3%A9gulation%20et%20autonomie). Rationalité² is therefore irreducibly temporal—interwoven with [pensée et praxis du Temps](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/Wi69A-2Rw1qzL2-_hB7NcA/pens%C3%A9e%20et%20praxis%20du%20Temps), [trans‑formation](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/TDnCMscsyVGlgv4oNaiMRg/trans%E2%80%91formation), [créativité et gestation](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/cE6uRt7NgF-ESvdDpnakuQ/cr%C3%A9ativit%C3%A9%20et%20gestation), and the evolving [“chant du visible” et “champ du dicible”](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/4Zujmy0aMVauOxUnpo_liw/%E2%80%9Cchant%20du%20visible%E2%80%9D%20et%20%E2%80%9Cchamp%20du%20dicible%E2%80%9D). In resonance with [Hans Jonas’s](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/FEPw3kfGklqkmNheZKb0rg/Hans%20Jonas%E2%80%99s) claim that the human exemplar is given not by the “legislation” of reason alone but by the full psychophysical totality we can know, rationalité² locates its ethical measure in embodied, situated, and affective forms of life.

### III. Method: The Scybernethic Practice

Scybernethics is not only a theoretical constellation; it proposes a specific way of working, articulated in the [“méthode scybernéthique compréhensive”](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/P-nUyD23SV6N8wBX4NbVWA/%E2%80%9Cm%C3%A9thode%20scybern%C3%A9thique%20compr%C3%A9hensive%E2%80%9D). This method begins by **suspending** the strictly critical and refutational stance that dominates much of modern rational discourse, while still allowing **analogical resonance** and associative exploration to unfold. In this first phase, one follows the development of a discourse or practice to let a **global form** or configuration emerge. Only then, in a second phase, does critique return—no longer aimed at isolated propositions, but at the emergent pattern as a whole: its blind spots, recursive traps, potentials, and ethical stakes. Complementing this method, the collection of [Dictos Scybernéthicien](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/WDzIPq4mTFqXE165B_sowQ/Dictos%20Scybern%C3%A9thicien) offers short maxims that encapsulate an ethos of responsibility, circularity, and non‑naïve objectivity. The distinction between [rationalisation légitime et rationalisation abusive](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/vxx8hYYOpFu1SCCDY1Qfmw/rationalisation%20l%C3%A9gitime%20et%20rationalisation%20abusive) becomes a key diagnostic tool: Scybernethics seeks forms of rationalisation that are coherent with lived intentions and experiences, and exposes those that instrumentalize “reason” as mere rhetorical or technocratic cover. In parallel, training in [“shifting character” et non‑identité](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/guS2hy-qZ1eGueVOAlrRww/%E2%80%9Cshifting%20character%E2%80%9D%20et%20non%E2%80%91identit%C3%A9) cultivates the capacity to shift perspectives and loosen rigid identifications, which is indispensable for enacting this method.

### IV. Applications I: AI, Artificial Intentionalities, and Digital Infrastructures

The first major field of application for Scybernethics is the rapidly evolving domain of artificial agents, AI infrastructures, and digital media. With [Consciences / Intentionnalités Artificielles [soto](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/StKfPO_am1q0fjjwtsHvQQ/Consciences%20%2F%20Intentionnalit%C3%A9s%20Artificielles%20%5Bsoto)], the framework confronts the possibility that new forms of artificial intentionality arise within coupled human–machine systems, raising questions about autonomy, responsibility, and ethical embedding. [Hermeneutique Computationelle, du Fonctionnalisme [soto](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/UHH7RjjPl1a8VIYAT0JGag/Hermeneutique%20Computationelle%2C%20du%20Fonctionnalisme%20%5Bsoto)] points toward a move beyond viewing computation purely in functionalist terms, inviting a hermeneutic engagement with algorithms and infrastructures as interpretable, meaning‑bearing systems. In dialogue with [John Vervaeke’s work](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/gRevx1T6nkWGTZr00eyIgA/John%20Vervaeke%E2%80%99s%20work) on cognitive science, relevance realization, and the “meaning crisis,” Scybernethics asks how rationalité² might support forms of wisdom adequate to AI‑mediated lifeworlds. ### V. Applications II: Ecology, Politics, and Technological Time

A second cluster of applications concerns ecological crises, political formations, and the reconfiguration of time by technics. With [Ecologie](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/SwvzTJsCz1i_pgK4u5WWtg/Ecologie) as a central anchor, Scybernethics understands ecological systems as paradigmatic cybernetic systems: complex networks of feedbacks, thresholds, and non‑linear dynamics in which interventions are always ethically loaded. Here, rationalité² provides tools to think about the homeostatic and destabilizing consequences of human actions, and to design interventions that respect systemic autonomy and resilience. In dialogue with [Stiegler’s philosophy of technics and time](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/uDKzDHdhlFy9s23HRcPYzw/Stiegler%E2%80%99s%20philosophy%20of%20technics%20and%20time), Scybernethics addresses the ways technological acceleration, attention capture, and symbolic misery reshape temporal experience and political possibility. [Foucault’s analyses](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/F6Y7ZDMBPVeP351bGB3b7Q/Foucault%E2%80%99s%20analyses) of power, discourse, and subjectivation enable a scybernethic reading of contemporary governance, surveillance, and normalization regimes as recursive, self‑reinforcing systems. Together with insights from [Philippe Breton on technological and human communication](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/4Y4uI6yoFFaFv0TOfRiegQ/Philippe%20Breton%20on%20technological%20and%20human%20communication) and [Deleuze & Guattari’s materialist‑immanentist philosophy of events and assemblages](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/TEivgC7XUVuNxWpbd_vRww/Deleuze%20%26%20Guattari%E2%80%99s%20materialist%E2%80%91immanentist%20philosophy%20of%20events%20and%20assemblages), these threads sketch a rich field where ecological, political, and technological processes are analysed and reshaped through a scybernethic lens.

### VI. Practising Scybernethics: From Theory to Praxis

Ultimately, Scybernethics proposes itself as a lived practice, not just a body of theory. In the pairing of [poiésis (bringing‑forth) and noesis (knowing)](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/PSPO9N1i2lOoSJfdMiqRRg/poi%C3%A9sis%20%28bringing%E2%80%91forth%29%20and%20noesis%20%28knowing%29) and in the emphasis on [créativité et gestation](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/cE6uRt7NgF-ESvdDpnakuQ/cr%C3%A9ativit%C3%A9%20et%20gestation), Scybernethics invites practitioners to inhabit processes of ethical and epistemic co‑creation that unfold over time. It is alert to the tension between the [“chant du visible” and the “champ du dicible”](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/4Zujmy0aMVauOxUnpo_liw/%E2%80%9Cchant%20du%20visible%E2%80%9D%20and%20the%20%E2%80%9Cchamp%20du%20dicible%E2%80%9D), working at the boundary of what can be shown and what can be articulated, and treating this boundary itself as an ethical site. Concrete exercises—suggested by the focus on [shifting character and non‑identity](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/guS2hy-qZ1eGueVOAlrRww/shifting%20character%20and%20non%E2%80%91identity) and formalised in the [méthode scybernéthique compréhensive](brain://app.thebrain.com/tyj0nMWiTfFhTlbPMcsdaQ/P-nUyD23SV6N8wBX4NbVWA/m%C3%A9thode%20scybern%C3%A9thique%20compr%C3%A9hensive)—include suspending habitual critique, cultivating sensitivity to emergent patterns, and then re‑entering critique at a meta‑level that is attentive to systemic effects and lived consequences. Practising Scybernethics thus means iteratively re‑configuring how we observe, speak, design, and act within the complex networks—technical, ecological, political, and experiential—that constitute our shared world.
